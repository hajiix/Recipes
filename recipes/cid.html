Canjeero iyo Dallac Balaash

History

Canjeero is one of the many types of flatbread that can be found in the horn of Africa. Though its origin is unknown, some theories suggest it was found between the 8th and 10th centuries. However, I believe it's been around longer than that, but it's hard to pin point an exact time because most of Somali history has only been recorded orally. Canjeero is a staple of Somali culture, and has been introduced to the rest of the world the past few decades by the Somali Diaspora. Dallac Balaash is essentialy a tomato soup. The literal translation of dallac is stir fry and balaash is having no worth. This is because in Somali culture, every soup must have some form of meat, but with dallac balaash, there isn't any meat so it's referred to as a fake soup with no meat(harsh i know)! As you can see, Somalis love their meat.

Ingredients

Canjeero

Starter Mixture:

  • ½ cup Sorghum flour
  • 1 cup White corn meal
  • 1 Tbsp Instant dry yeast
  • 1 cup Water (lukewarm)

Add the Starter to the Following:

  • 4 cups Self-rising flour (Bur canjeero)
  • ¼ cup Sugar
  • 4¼ cups Water (lukewarm)

Basic Dallac Balaash:

  • 3 tablespoons (45 milliliters) olive oil
  • 1 medium onion (finely diced)
  • 3 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 4 medium tomatoes (peeled & diced)
  • ½ teaspoon (2.5 milliliters) salt
  • ¼ teaspoon (1.25 milliliters) ground black pepper

Dallac Balaash with Extra Ingredients:

  • 3 tablespoons (45 milliliters) olive oil
  • 1 medium onion (finely diced)
  • 3 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 4 medium tomatoes (peeled & diced)
  • ½ teaspoon (2.5 milliliters) salt
  • 2 tablespoons (30 milliliters) tomato paste
  • ¼ cup (32 grams) grated carrots
  • 1 jalapeno pepper (finely diced, seeded)
  • ¼ cup (45 grams) green bell peppers (finely diced)
  • 1 teaspoon (5 milliliters) xawaash
  • ½ cup (118 milliliters) water
  • ¼ cup (40 grams) fresh cilantro (chopped)
  • ¼ teaspoon (1.25 milliliters) ground black pepper

How to make Canjeero iyo Dallac Balaash Step-By-Step

  1. Using medium heat, fry the onions in the olive oil for 3 minutes then add the minced garlic and cook for 1 more minute.
  2. Add the chopped tomatoes, salt and tomato paste, then stir well.
  3. Add the carrots, jalapeno peppers, bell pepper, xawaash spice mix. Stir after each addition, then add the water and stir well.
  4. Cover and cook over low heat for 10 minutes.
  5. Add the chopped, fresh cilantro
  6. Add the ground black pepper, stir well and turn off the heat.
